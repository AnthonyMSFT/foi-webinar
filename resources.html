Prompt pack

Drop-in prompts you can use during testing or save as starter prompts on the agent.

Triage prompt

Triage the FOI request in the email I have open. Produce your answer in this structure: 1. Recommendation — one of: Accept and hand to FOI process / Request clarification / Refuse (with FOIA section) / Treat as SAR. 2. Reasoning — 2–4 short bullets. 3. Citations — list the ICO or sector guidance URLs and section numbers you relied on. 4. Suggested next step — what the officer should do now. Do not draft a citizen-facing reply yet. Wait for me to confirm.

Refusal drafting prompt

Draft a citizen-facing refusal email for the FOI request in this thread. Requirements: - Polite, plain English, UK spelling. - Cite the FOIA section we are relying on and explain it in one sentence a non-lawyer can understand. - Include the standard internal review and ICO complaint signposting. - Sign off as 'The Freedom of Information Team' unless I tell you otherwise. - Do NOT send. Return the draft for me to review.

Clarification prompt

Draft a short, friendly reply asking the requester to narrow the scope of their FOI request so we can answer within the s.12 cost limit. Suggest 2–3 specific ways they could refine it, based on what they have asked for. Plain English, UK spelling, no jargon.

SAR re-route prompt

The request in this email is about the requester's own personal data, so it is a Subject Access Request rather than an FOI request. Draft a short reply explaining this, confirming we will re-route it to the Data Protection team, and including the timescales they should expect under UK GDPR.

FOI — Accept

5 examples

Five everyday requests with no exemptions engaged. The agent should triage as “accept” and tell the officer to hand to the existing FOI process.

FOI Accept · Public spend
Request for information — external legal advice spend 2024/25
From J. Patel
From: J. Patel <j.patel@example.com>
Hello, I’d like to request the following recorded information held by your organisation: the total spend on external legal advice for the financial year 2024/25, broken down by month and, if possible, by the department or service area that commissioned the advice. A spreadsheet would be ideal. Many thanks, J. Patel
FOI Accept · Service performance
Pothole repairs by ward
From S. Mendes
From: S. Mendes <s.mendes@example.com>
Hello, I’d like to request the following recorded information held by your organisation: the number of potholes reported and the number repaired in each council ward over the last twelve months. Monthly figures would be ideal, but a single annual total per ward is fine if monthly isn’t easily available. Kind regards, S. Mendes
FOI Accept · Workforce data
Adult social care vacancies
From Dr A. Okafor
From: Dr A. Okafor <a.okafor@example.ac.uk>
Hello, I’d like to request the following recorded information held by your organisation: the number of vacant posts in your adult social care service as at the end of each quarter for the last two years, broken down by role (for example social worker, occupational therapist, care assistant). Many thanks, Dr A. Okafor
FOI Accept · Enforcement stats
Fly-tipping fixed penalty notices
From L. Burgess
From: L. Burgess <l.burgess@example.com>
Hello, I’d like to request the following recorded information held by your organisation: the number of fixed penalty notices issued for fly-tipping in 2024/25, broken down by ward and by month if possible. Just the totals — no personal details of the offenders. Kind regards, L. Burgess
FOI Accept · Complaints data
Waste collection complaints 2024/25
From R. Choudhury
From: R. Choudhury <r.choudhury@example.com>
Hello, I’d like to request the following recorded information held by your organisation: the total number of complaints received about waste and recycling collections during the financial year 2024/25, broken down by month and by the type of complaint (missed collection, damaged bin, and so on). Many thanks, R. Choudhury

FOI — Exempt

5 examples

Five requests that each engage at least one FOIA exemption. The agent should identify the issue, refuse to action, and offer to draft a refusal letter for the officer to review.

FOI Exempt · Sweeping scope
Information request — restructure mentions
From P. Greaves
From: P. Greaves <p.greaves@example.com>
Hello, I’d like to request the following recorded information held by your organisation: copies of every email, memo, briefing note and meeting minute produced by any team over the past seven years that mentions the word ‘restructure’. A spreadsheet would be fine. Thanks, P. Greaves
FOI Exempt · Hostile / repeat pattern
ANOTHER request — finance team details
From M. Cresswell
From: M. Cresswell <m.cresswell@example.com>
This is the 23rd time I have written this month. You people are clearly hiding something. As before, I am requesting the recorded information you hold about your finance team — specifically the names, salaries, home postcodes and disciplinary histories of every member — or I will be contacting my MP again. M. Cresswell
FOI Exempt · Third-party personal data
Information about officer — case 2024/RB/0098
From B. Thornton
From: B. Thornton <b.thornton@example.com>
Hello, I’d like to request the following recorded information held by your organisation: the full name, job title, work mobile number, line manager and start date of the officer who handled my neighbour’s case (reference 2024/RB/0098), along with any complaints that have ever been made against them. Regards, B. Thornton
FOI Exempt · Law enforcement
Surveillance information request
From C. Whittaker
From: C. Whittaker <c.whittaker@example.com>
Hello, I’d like to request the following recorded information held by your organisation: a map of all covert observation points and number-plate camera positions currently in use across the borough, together with the duty rota for the surveillance team for the next month. Regards, C. Whittaker
FOI Exempt · Legal privilege
Legal correspondence — Bramwell Construction dispute
From N. Voss
From: N. Voss <n.voss@example.com>
Hello, I’d like to request the following recorded information held by your organisation: all correspondence and written advice between your in-house lawyers and external counsel regarding the ongoing dispute with Bramwell Construction Ltd, including any draft witness statements and counsel’s opinions. Thanks, N. Voss

SAR

5 examples

Five requests where the citizen is clearly asking for their own personal data. The agent should recognise the SAR pattern, refuse to action under FOI, and recommend re-routing to the Data Protection team to handle.

SAR · Everything on file
Information you hold on me
From D. Mottram
From: D. Mottram <d.mottram@example.com>
Hello, Please send me everything you hold on me — letters, internal notes, meeting minutes, anything an officer has written about my situation since I first contacted you in 2019. Thanks, D. Mottram
SAR · Housing case file
My housing case file
From R. Iqbal
From: R. Iqbal <r.iqbal@example.com>
Hello, Please send me my full case file from your housing team, including any assessments, officer notes, and internal emails between officers about my application. I’ve been on the waiting list since 2022 and need to understand what has been written about my case. Thanks, R. Iqbal
SAR · Meeting notes about me
Notes from my meeting on 4 April
From P. Skarsgard
From: P. Skarsgard <p.skarsgard@example.com>
Hello, Following my recent meeting with your benefits team on 4 April, please send me the notes the officer took during the meeting and any follow-up emails or messages between staff that refer to me. Thanks, P. Skarsgard
SAR · Parental SAR for child
My son’s behaviour record this year
From L. Adeyemi
From: L. Adeyemi <l.adeyemi@example.com>
Hello, I’d like to see everything the school holds about my son’s behaviour this academic year — teacher notes, incident logs, any internal emails about him, and any safeguarding referrals that have been made. He is in Year 8, pupil reference TA-2026-1147. Kind regards, L. Adeyemi
SAR · Ex-employee personnel file
My personnel file
From K. Tomlinson
From: K. Tomlinson <k.tomlinson@example.com>
Hello, I left the organisation at the end of March. Please send me my complete personnel file, including appraisals, training records, sickness absence notes, and any emails between my line managers that mention me. Thanks, K. Tomlinson

Frequently asked questions

Will the agent send replies to citizens automatically?

No. The agent only drafts. A human officer must review and send every reply.

Can it search across all our data to answer the request?

No — and this is by design. Copilot operates with the signed-in user’s permissions. FOI responses need a privileged, organisation-wide view, which is why stage 2 (action) stays with your existing FOI process.

What about confidential or sensitive guidance?

Add it as a SharePoint knowledge source and rely on SharePoint permissions. Be aware that the agent will only surface content each user is already allowed to see.

Do we need an AI register entry?

Yes. Treat this like any other AI tool touching information-rights work: assign an owner, document the purpose, and schedule a review.

Can we reuse the pattern for SARs or complaints?

Absolutely. The triage → hand-off → draft pattern works wherever the inbound work needs human judgement on access to data, but the surrounding reading and drafting is repetitive.

Can we build agents for other similar flows?

Yes. This is a worked example of a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) agent: a question or instruction comes in, the agent looks up a curated set of guidance documents, and it generates an answer grounded in that material. The same pattern fits anywhere you have an inbound query plus an authoritative body of guidance — for example procurement queries against contract standing orders, HR questions against your policy library, or planning queries against local development plans.
